CONNECT - Talent Upskilling & Alignment

CONNECT is the culmination of hundreds of designs, thousands of hours of internal and user-facing discovery, and countless iterations of development and refinement. What started as a tool to provide job-seekers with a clear path to job qualification, eventually became the job-seeker empowering and employer enabling supertool that it is today.

Starting in 2020, the first iterations of a job transparency and alignment solution began to form. Beginning as individual instances of learning academies geared toward individual companies, user research and market testing would lead to what would eventually become CONNECT.

Wireframe Design

CONNECT - Wireframes/Initial Design

The following images come from the very first round of design ideation and proof of concept for CONNECT.

The initial drive for what eventually became CONNECT was to create a tool that would aid an individual in qualifying for a specified role in a company as well as providing a clear path to success. As seen in following examples, we used Zoom for a test case. Users would be prompted to identify their persona as a user of Zoom. They would then be provided with a clear learning path comprised of skills indicated as being critical to successfully using Zoom in that role.

Low-Fidelity Design

CONNECT - Wireframes/Initial Design

The images below are snippets of the designs for a hiring transparency tool. These design were the product of user and market testing following our initial academy-style upskilling tool.

Aimed at providing a clear candidate view to employers and recruiters, this iteration of designs presented a workflow through which employers would be able to search for candidates based on a percentage of "skills alignment." Candidates would be asked to make assertions about there skillset, while employers would set skills requirements for their open positions. These data points would allow the tool to indicate a level of alignment for each candidate, allowing employers to prioritize those candidates that were the best match for the role.

Evolving from an academy-style upskilling tool, we learned we were missing the mark by simply providing job-centric upskilling. After speaking to countless employers and recruiters, it became clear that the cost and challenge of making poor hiring decisions made based on the skills assertions of applicants wasn't going to be fixed with our current solution. We needed to transform our current approach and move toward candidate metrics and objectivity to assist the employer in making quality hires.

Live Product Design

CONNECT - Live Product Design

The next several images represent the latest iteration of designs, as well as the fully-developed and live product. This app, called CONNECT, is undergoing its initial launch with the Navajo Nation.

CONNECT is more than just a talent matching tool. Likewise, it goes beyond simple upskilling. Combining the best of both worlds, CONNECT serves two sides of the same market. Employers are able to post jobs in a new fassion, using provided skills and proficiency levels to indicate the requirements for success in their listed role. Likewise, job-seekers are empowered to build skills profiles, make assertions, and attach certifications, artifacts, and work-products as evidence of their claims. Employers see the strongest candidates, and job-seekers see the jobs where they are the closest match. Likewise, job-seekers are shown their current deficiencies and provided with suggested learning resources and activities to help them upskill and increase their overall alignment with their desired job.

Additional user testing and market analysis showed that neither the academy-style upskilling tool or the employer empowerment platform were the correct solution. That is, they did not solve the problem individually. What we came to realize was that our users were not just job-seekers, and not just employers. In actuality, the market we were aiming to assist is comprised of both persona types. This realization lead us to understand that both solutions were not only required, but harmonious in addressing the needs of both job-seekers and employers as it pertains to finding quality employment and hiring qualified candidates. It was from this undestanding and validation from the market that CONNECT came into existence.
